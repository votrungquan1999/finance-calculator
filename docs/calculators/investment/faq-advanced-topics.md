# Investment Calculator FAQ - Advanced Topics

## Overview

This document covers advanced investment topics including asset allocation, active vs passive investing, portfolio management across multiple accounts, and retirement readiness assessment.

## Advanced Topics FAQ (Q21-Q25)

### Q21: How do I choose the right asset allocation?

**Answer**: Asset allocation depends on your risk tolerance and time horizon:

- Age-based rule: 100 - age = % in stocks
- Risk tolerance: Conservative, moderate, aggressive
- Time horizon: Longer = more aggressive
- Consider your ability to handle volatility

**Age-Based Allocation**:

```
Age 25: 75% stocks, 25% bonds
Age 35: 65% stocks, 35% bonds
Age 45: 55% stocks, 45% bonds
Age 55: 45% stocks, 55% bonds
Age 65: 35% stocks, 65% bonds
```

**Risk Tolerance Factors**:

- Investment experience
- Time horizon
- Financial situation
- Emotional ability to handle volatility

**Next Steps**:

1. Determine your risk tolerance
2. Consider your time horizon
3. Choose appropriate allocation
4. Review and adjust regularly

### Q22: What's the difference between active and passive investing?

**Answer**: Key differences:

- Active: Trying to beat the market, higher fees
- Passive: Matching market performance, lower fees
- Index funds: Passive, low-cost
- Mutual funds: Can be active or passive
- ETFs: Usually passive, low-cost

**Cost Comparison**:

```
Active mutual fund: 1-2% annual fee
Index fund: 0.1-0.5% annual fee
Difference: 0.5-1.9% per year
```

**Performance Reality**:

- Most active funds underperform their benchmarks
- Lower fees = higher net returns
- Passive investing is often more effective
- Consider your investment knowledge

**Next Steps**:

1. Understand the difference
2. Consider your investment knowledge
3. Choose appropriate approach
4. Focus on low-cost options

### Q23: How do I handle multiple investment accounts?

**Answer**: Coordinate across all accounts:

- Treat as one portfolio
- Consider tax implications
- Optimize asset location
- Rebalance across accounts
- Keep track of all contributions

**Asset Location Strategy**:

```
Taxable: Tax-efficient investments (index funds)
401k: All investments (tax-deferred)
IRA: All investments (tax-deferred)
Roth: Growth investments (tax-free)
```

**Coordination Tips**:

- Use spreadsheet or app to track
- Rebalance across all accounts
- Consider tax implications
- Optimize contribution allocation

**Next Steps**:

1. List all investment accounts
2. Determine total asset allocation
3. Optimize asset location
4. Rebalance across accounts

### Q24: What if I change jobs frequently?

**Answer**: Plan for job changes:

- Understand vesting schedules
- Consider 401k rollover options
- Don't lose employer match
- Consolidate accounts when possible
- Keep track of all accounts

**Vesting Schedules**:

```
Immediate: 100% vested immediately
Cliff: 100% vested after X years
Graded: Gradual vesting over time
```

**Rollover Options**:

- Roll to new 401k
- Roll to IRA
- Leave in old 401k (if allowed)
- Cash out (not recommended)

**Next Steps**:

1. Understand vesting schedules
2. Plan for job changes
3. Consider rollover options
4. Keep track of all accounts

### Q25: How do I know if I'm on track for retirement?

**Answer**: Use multiple benchmarks:

- 15-20% of income saved
- 1x salary by age 30
- 3x salary by age 40
- 6x salary by age 50
- 8x salary by age 60

**Retirement Readiness Benchmarks**:

```
Age 30: 1x annual salary
Age 40: 3x annual salary
Age 50: 6x annual salary
Age 60: 8x annual salary
Age 67: 10x annual salary
```

**Additional Factors**:

- Social Security benefits
- Pension benefits
- Healthcare costs
- Lifestyle expectations
- Inflation

**Next Steps**:

1. Calculate your current savings rate
2. Compare to benchmarks
3. Adjust if behind
4. Review annually
