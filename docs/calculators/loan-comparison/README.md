## Overview

The Loan Comparison Calculator allows users to compare all three loan calculation methods side-by-side: Annuity Method (fixed payment), Declining Balance Method (decreasing payment), and Fee Analysis Method (APR with fees). This helps users understand which method works best for their specific situation.

## Detailed Method Comparison

<details>
<summary><strong>ğŸ”„ Annuity Method (Fixed Payment)</strong></summary>

**Characteristics:**

- Same payment amount every period
- Early payments mostly interest
- Later payments mostly principal
- Most familiar to US borrowers

**Advantages:**

- Predictable monthly payments
- Easy to budget and plan
- Most lenders offer this structure
- Simple to understand

**Disadvantages:**

- Higher total interest paid
- Less flexible payment structure
- May not optimize for borrower's situation

**Best For:**

- Mortgages and auto loans
- Borrowers who need predictable payments
- Standard consumer lending
- First-time borrowers

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>ğŸ“‰ Declining Balance Method (Decreasing Payment)</strong></summary>

**Characteristics:**

- Higher initial payments
- Decreasing payments over time
- More principal paid early
- Less interest paid overall

**Advantages:**

- Lower total interest paid
- Faster principal reduction
- Better for borrowers with decreasing income
- More efficient use of money

**Disadvantages:**

- Higher initial payment burden
- Less common in US lending
- May be difficult to qualify for
- Requires higher initial income

**Best For:**

- Business loans
- Borrowers with high initial income
- Those who want to minimize interest
- International lending

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>ğŸ’° Fee Analysis Method (APR with Fees)</strong></summary>

**Characteristics:**

- Includes all loan costs in APR
- Accounts for points and fees
- Shows true cost of borrowing
- Enables accurate comparison

**Advantages:**

- True cost comparison
- Includes all fees and costs
- Prevents hidden cost surprises
- Regulatory compliance

**Disadvantages:**

- More complex calculations
- Requires detailed fee information
- May not be offered by all lenders
- Can be confusing for borrowers

**Best For:**

- Loans with significant fees
- Comparing different loan offers
- Understanding true borrowing costs
- Regulatory compliance

</details>

## Decision Framework

### ğŸ¯ Choosing the Right Method

<details>
<summary><strong>âœ… Choose Annuity Method When:</strong></summary>

- **Predictable Income:** Stable monthly income
- **Budget Planning:** Need consistent payments
- **Standard Lending:** Most US consumer loans
- **Simplicity:** Want straightforward structure
- **First-Time Borrowing:** New to loan management

**Example Scenarios:**

- First-time homebuyer
- Standard auto loan
- Personal loan for debt consolidation
- Student loan refinancing

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>âœ… Choose Declining Balance When:</strong></summary>

- **High Initial Income:** Can afford higher early payments
- **Interest Minimization:** Want to save on interest
- **Business Lending:** Common in business loans
- **Decreasing Income:** Expect income to decrease over time
- **Long-Term Planning:** Focus on total cost, not monthly payment

**Example Scenarios:**

- Business equipment loan
- Real estate investment loan
- High-income borrower
- International lending

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>âœ… Choose Fee Analysis When:</strong></summary>

- **Complex Fee Structure:** Multiple fees and points
- **Loan Comparison:** Comparing different offers
- **Regulatory Compliance:** Need accurate APR disclosure
- **True Cost Analysis:** Want complete cost picture
- **Professional Lending:** Commercial or investment loans

**Example Scenarios:**

- Mortgage with points
- Commercial real estate loan
- Loan refinancing analysis
- Regulatory compliance

</details>
