## Strategy Comparison

<details>
<summary><strong>âš¡ Avalanche Method (Highest Interest First)</strong></summary>

**How it works:**

1. List all debts by interest rate (highest first)
2. Pay minimums on all debts
3. Apply extra payment to highest interest debt
4. When paid off, move to next highest rate

**Benefits:**

- Saves the most interest
- Pays off debt fastest
- Mathematically optimal

**Best for:**

- People motivated by numbers
- Those who want maximum savings
- Long-term financial optimization

**Example:**

- Credit Card A: $5,000 @ 24% (min $100)
- Credit Card B: $3,000 @ 18% (min $60)
- Personal Loan: $10,000 @ 12% (min $200)
- Extra payment: $200/month

**Avalanche order:** Card A â†’ Card B â†’ Personal Loan

</details>

**How it works:**

1. List all debts by balance (lowest first)

3. Apply extra payment to lowest balance debt
4. When paid off, move to next lowest balance

**Benefits:**

- Quick psychological wins
- Builds momentum and confidence
- Simplifies debt management

**Best for:**

- People who need motivation
- Those overwhelmed by debt
- Anyone who benefits from quick wins

**Example:**
