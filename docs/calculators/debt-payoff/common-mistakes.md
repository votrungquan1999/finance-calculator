# Debt Payoff Calculator Common Mistakes

## Overview

This document covers the most common mistakes users make when using the Debt Payoff Calculator, with detailed explanations, real-world examples, and actionable solutions.

## Debt Payoff Calculator Common Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Choosing Wrong Strategy

**The Problem**: Not understanding avalanche vs snowball strategies.

**Why It's Wrong**:

- Different strategies for different goals
- May not optimize interest savings
- Poor strategy selection
- Misses optimization opportunities

**The Solution**: Understand both strategies and choose based on your goals.

**Real Example**:

- Avalanche saves more interest
- Snowball provides psychological motivation
- Choose based on your needs

**How to Avoid**:

- Understand both strategies
- Consider your psychology
- Factor in interest savings
- Choose what works for you

### Mistake 2: Not Considering Minimum Payments

**The Problem**: Forgetting about minimum payments on other debts.

**Why It's Wrong**:

- Must pay minimums on all debts
- Affects available extra payment
- Poor planning
- May miss payments

**The Solution**: Always account for minimum payments on all debts.

**Real Example**:

- $500 extra payment available
- But $300 needed for minimums
- Only $200 available for extra payment

**How to Avoid**:

- List all minimum payments
- Calculate available extra payment
- Plan accordingly
- Don't miss minimum payments

### Mistake 3: Not Tracking Progress

**The Problem**: Not monitoring debt payoff progress.

**Why It's Wrong**:

- Misses motivation opportunities
- May lose momentum
- Poor accountability
- Ineffective strategy

**The Solution**: Track progress regularly and celebrate milestones.

**Real Example**:

- Track monthly progress
- Celebrate each debt paid off
- Adjust strategy as needed
- Stay motivated

**How to Avoid**:

- Set up tracking system
- Review progress monthly
- Celebrate milestones
- Adjust strategy as needed
