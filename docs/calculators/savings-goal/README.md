## Priority-Based Allocation

### ğŸ¯ Priority Levels

| Priority     | Description                      | Allocation        | Example                      |
| ------------ | -------------------------------- | ----------------- | ---------------------------- |
| **Critical** | Essential for financial security | 50-70% of savings | Emergency fund, debt payoff  |
| **High**     | Important life goals             | 20-30% of savings | Home down payment, education |
| **Medium**   | Desirable but flexible           | 10-20% of savings | Vacation, home renovation    |
| **Low**      | Nice to have                     | 0-10% of savings  | Luxury items, entertainment  |

### ğŸ“Š Allocation Strategies

<details>
<summary><strong>ğŸ’° Fixed Percentage Method</strong></summary>

**How it works:**

- Assign fixed percentages to each priority level
- Allocate monthly savings based on percentages
- Adjust as goals are completed

**Example:**

- Critical: 60% ($600/month)
- High: 25% ($250/month)
- Medium: 15% ($150/month)

**Best for:** Consistent income, stable goals

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>ğŸ¯ Goal-First Method</strong></summary>

**How it works:**

- Focus on one goal at a time
- Complete highest priority goal first
- Move to next goal when first is complete

**Example:**

1. Emergency fund ($10,000) - 6 months
2. Home down payment ($50,000) - 2 years
3. Vacation ($5,000) - 6 months

**Best for:** Focused approach, limited resources

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>âš–ï¸ Balanced Method</strong></summary>

**How it works:**

- Allocate based on goal urgency and importance
- Consider timeline and priority together
- Adjust monthly based on progress

**Example:**

- Urgent + High Priority: 40%
- Important + Medium Timeline: 30%
- Desirable + Long Timeline: 20%
- Flexible + Low Priority: 10%

**Best for:** Multiple goals, varying timelines

</details>
