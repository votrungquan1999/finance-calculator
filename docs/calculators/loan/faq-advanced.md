# Loan Calculator FAQ - Advanced Topics

## Overview

This document covers advanced loan topics including credit considerations, loan applications, closing process, and refinancing strategies.

## Advanced Topics FAQ (Q15-Q20)

### Q15: How do I calculate loan-to-value ratio?

**Answer**: LTV calculation:

- LTV = Loan amount รท Property value
- Higher LTV = higher risk = higher rate
- Lower LTV = lower risk = lower rate
- Aim for LTV < 80% to avoid PMI

**LTV Example**:

```
Property value: $400,000
Loan amount: $320,000
LTV: $320,000 รท $400,000 = 80%
PMI required: Yes (LTV > 80%)
```

**LTV Impact on Rates**:

- LTV < 80%: Best rates
- LTV 80-90%: Slightly higher rates
- LTV > 90%: Higher rates + PMI

**Next Steps**:

1. Calculate your LTV
2. Consider larger down payment
3. Shop for best rates
4. Factor in PMI costs

### Q16: What if I have bad credit?

**Answer**: Options for bad credit:

- Improve credit score first
- Consider FHA loans (lower credit requirements)
- Find co-signer
- Save larger down payment
- Shop for subprime lenders

**Credit Score Impact**:

```
Excellent (740+): Best rates
Good (680-739): Good rates
Fair (620-679): Higher rates
Poor (<620): Much higher rates
```

**Improving Credit Score**:

- Pay bills on time
- Reduce credit utilization
- Don't open new accounts
- Check credit report for errors

**Next Steps**:

1. Check your credit score
2. Improve credit if possible
3. Consider FHA loans
4. Shop around for best terms

### Q17: How do I handle multiple loan applications?

**Answer**: Multiple applications strategy:

- Apply within 30-day window (counts as one inquiry)
- Get pre-approved, not just pre-qualified
- Compare all offers before deciding
- Don't apply to too many lenders

**Application Timeline**:

```
Week 1: Get pre-approved
Week 2: Shop for rates
Week 3: Compare offers
Week 4: Choose lender
```

**Best Practices**:

- Apply within 30 days
- Get pre-approved first
- Compare all offers
- Don't over-apply

**Next Steps**:

1. Get pre-approved
2. Shop within 30 days
3. Compare all offers
4. Choose best deal

### Q18: What if I want to pay off loan early?

**Answer**: Early payoff considerations:

- Check for prepayment penalties
- Calculate interest savings
- Consider opportunity cost
- Factor in tax benefits

**Early Payoff Example**:

```
$300,000 loan, 6.5%, 30 years:
- Regular payoff: $382,320 interest
- 15-year payoff: $170,000 interest
- Interest saved: $212,320
- Monthly payment: $2,611 vs $1,896
```

**Early Payoff Strategies**:

- Make extra payments
- Choose shorter term
- Make bi-weekly payments
- Use windfalls

**Next Steps**:

### Q19: How do I handle loan closing?

**Answer**: Closing process:

- Review all documents carefully
- Bring required documents
- Ask questions about anything unclear
- Don't sign if you don't understand

**Closing Checklist**:

```
Documents to bring:
- ID and proof of income
- Bank statements
- Insurance information
- Down payment funds
```

**Closing Costs**:

- Origination fees
- Title insurance
- Appraisal
- Inspection
- Attorney fees

**Next Steps**:

1. Prepare required documents
2. Review closing disclosure
3. Ask questions
4. Sign documents

### Q20: What if I need to refinance later?

**Answer**: Refinancing considerations:

- Check for prepayment penalties
- Calculate break-even point
- Consider closing costs
- Shop for best rates

**Refinance Example**:

```
Current: $280,000, 6.5%, 25 years left
New: $280,000, 5.5%, 30 years
Closing costs: $5,000
Monthly savings: $150
Break-even: 33 months
```

**When to Refinance**:

- Rate drop of 0.5% or more
- Break-even within 2-3 years
- Planning to stay in home
- Closing costs reasonable

**Next Steps**:

1. Monitor interest rates
2. Calculate break-even point
3. Shop for best rates
4. Consider all costs
