## Method Comparison

<details>
<summary><strong>üîÑ Annuity Method (Fixed Payment)</strong></summary>

**Best for:** Most US loans, mortgages, auto loans, personal loans

**Features:**

- Same payment amount every period
- Early payments mostly interest, later payments mostly principal
- Most familiar to US borrowers
- Easy to budget and plan

**Use when:**

- Getting a standard mortgage or auto loan
- Need predictable monthly payments
- Comparing different loan offers
- Planning monthly budget

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>üìâ Declining Balance Method (Decreasing Payment)</strong></summary>

**Best for:** Business loans, some international loans, when you want to save interest

**Features:**

- Higher initial payments, lower later payments
- Saves significant interest over loan life
- Requires higher initial payment capacity
- Less common in US consumer lending

**Use when:**

- Getting a business loan
- Want to minimize total interest paid
- Have higher initial payment capacity
- Planning for decreasing income over time

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>üí∞ Fee Analysis Method (APR with Fees)</strong></summary>

**Best for:** Loans with points, origination fees, or complex fee structures

**Features:**

- Calculates true APR including all fees
- Compares loans with different fee structures
- Shows break-even analysis for points
- Accounts for all loan costs

**Use when:**

- Comparing loans with different fees
- Deciding whether to pay points
- Analyzing refinancing options
- Understanding true loan cost

</details>
