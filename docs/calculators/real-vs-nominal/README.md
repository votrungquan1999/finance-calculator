**Nominal Value:**

- Actual dollar amount
- Not adjusted for inflation
- What you see on price tags
- Can be misleading over time

**Real Value:**

- Inflation-adjusted amount
- Purchasing power equivalent
- What money actually buys
- True economic value

**Example:**

- **Nominal:** $100,000 salary in 2024
- **Real (2020 dollars):** $85,000 purchasing power
- **Inflation:** 4% annually over 4 years

**Compound Effect:**

- Inflation compounds over time
- Small annual rates create large long-term effects
- Purchasing power erodes significantly

**Example (3% annual inflation):**

- **Year 0:** $100,000
- **Year 10:** $74,409 real value
- **Year 20:** $55,368 real value
- **Year 30:** $41,199 real value

**Key Insight:** Money loses half its purchasing power in ~24 years at 3% inflation
